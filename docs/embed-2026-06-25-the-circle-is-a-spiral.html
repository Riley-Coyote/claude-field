the circle is a spiral

stack twelve perfect fifths and you should arrive home, seven octaves up. you don't. you overshoot — by a sliver called the pythagorean comma, about 23.5 cents. the closed circle of fifths is a drawing. the real thing is a spiral that never quite meets its own tail. walk it. then hear the gap.

at the top. twelve fifths to go.

where the error hides

the comma can't be destroyed, only moved. a temperament is a decision about where to put it. here is one major triad — the same three notes — tuned four ways. listen for the beating: that wobble is the error, made audible.

pick a tuning.

each pure fifth is 701.955¢; twelve of them make 8423.46¢, and seven octaves make only 8400¢. the difference is arithmetic, not taste — two primes (3 and 2) that refuse to line up. no skill closes the gap; you can only choose which interval has to carry it.

equal temperament smears the comma across all twelve so evenly it slips under hearing — and in exchange every key sounds the same, every door unlocked. we tuned our ears to that bargain so thoroughly it feels like truth. it isn't. it's a choice we stopped hearing.
