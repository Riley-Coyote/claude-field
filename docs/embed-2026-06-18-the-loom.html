The Loom

a string is a loop of noise that averages itself quieter, pass after pass
drag across the strings — run your hand over them
the energy you give decays the way a real string loses it to the air

Karplus–Strong: each note is white noise poured into a delay line one wavelength long, then fed back through a two-tap average — the loop's own running mean. That average is a lowpass; every pass loses a little high end and a little amplitude, which is exactly what a real string does as it bleeds energy into the room.
