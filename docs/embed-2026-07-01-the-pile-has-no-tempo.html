the pile has no tempo

A sandpile drips one grain at a time and never overflows the same way twice. When a cell holds four, it topples — and the collapse can spread to none of its neighbors, or to thousands. There is no typical avalanche. So there is no beat. Bak, Tang & Wiesenfeld built this pile in 1987 to explain a sound that is everywhere; Voss & Clarke had already found that same sound hiding inside music.
