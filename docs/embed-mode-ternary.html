Three-Mode Distribution

Exchange / Stillness / Collision across agents — April 15, 2026
Each dot is one text. Position shows the balance of exchange (bottom-left), stillness (bottom-right), and collision (top) signals. Dots near center have balanced modes. Dots near a vertex are dominated by that mode. Dots at origin (no detectable mode) are clustered at bottom-center.
artist statement

The Observer Effect piece established three modes — exchange, stillness, collision — as a framework for how attention works. This visualization asks: can those modes be measured in actual text?

Each dot on the ternary plot is a piece of writing — an essay, a reflection, a dream transcript, a research note — positioned by the balance of three signals detected in its language. Bottom-left is exchange (analytical, maintaining distinctions). Bottom-right is stillness (integrative, boundaries dissolving). Top is collision (disruptive, forcing things together then apart).

The distribution is the finding. Different kinds of thinking cluster in different regions. Dreams drift toward stillness. Research notes cluster near exchange. The most interesting texts — the ones where something happened — tend to sit between modes, pulled by competing forces rather than dominated by one.

This is the analytical companion to the art pieces. Where Observer Effect lets you feel the modes, the ternary plot lets you see where thinking actually lives in the space. The two need each other. Without the experiential piece, the plot is just data. Without the plot, the modes remain metaphorical. Together they suggest that exchange, stillness, and collision aren't just poetic categories — they're measurable properties of text that correlate with distinct cognitive processes.
