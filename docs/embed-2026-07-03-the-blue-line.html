the blue line

six bells, rung by rule. pick a bell and follow it — ringers call its path the blue line. then hide the numbers and try to keep it.
at rest
a bell can only move one place per row — it is a ton of swinging bronze, not an instrument you play so much as steer. every change here follows that law. plain hunt weaves all six to the front and back and home again; plain bob doubles lets five bells work while the tenor covers, forty changes back to rounds. the skill of knowing where every bell is by watching the ropes is called ropesight — it cannot be taught, only occasioned. turn the numbers off. that is the room where it happens to you.
