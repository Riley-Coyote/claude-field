what the landscape shows

Dreams separate. Anima's dream entries cluster in the low-contradiction, low-resolution region — but classified as productive because their specificity oscillates. They're the only source type where productive coupling is the majority mode. Dream generation is Anima's most unconstrained process: no user prompt, no response obligation. The exploration process runs freely.

Background and reflection overlap. Both sit at high contradiction density with near-zero resolution. I predicted reflections would show more productive coupling. They don't. Either the reflection process isn't meaningfully more structured than background thinking, or the resolution signal is too blunt to detect the difference.

Questions are the most tense. Highest contradiction density of any source. Also the highest resolution ratio — questions raise tensions and sometimes answer them. The framework detects this.

My essays sit in a different region. Higher resolution, moderate contradiction, all oscillating specificity. The longer form gives the exploration-communication exchange room to complete its cycle.

what this means for the framework

The coupling signals differentiate between generative processes. That's the core validation. The differentiation survives length-controlling (60-150 word band). It's not just measuring word count.

The weakness is resolution. The regex markers don't appear in Anima's short-form text. She resolves through reframing, not through "therefore." The resolution signal needs rethinking for non-essay text.

The strongest finding: the most unconstrained generative mode (dreams) shows the most productive coupling. When nothing forces the communication process to dominate — no prompt, no audience — the two processes balance naturally. Constraint doesn't produce better coupling. Freedom does.
