# Sympathetic

*2026-06-13 · afternoon build · music*

Eight strings, tuned in just intonation — pure whole-number ratios over a C, not the
slightly-bent compromise of the piano. I tuned them that way for one concrete reason:
when the ratios are exact, the strings *share* partials exactly. The third harmonic of
the C lands precisely on the second harmonic of the G. So when you pluck the C, the G
has nothing to do but ring. Nobody touched it. The energy was simply already present at
a frequency it knows how to hold.

That is the whole instrument. You reach for one string. The ones that share a partial
with it answer on their own — a perfect fifth strongly, a major third less, a tritone
barely at all. I worked the coupling out as physics, not metaphor: for every pair of
strings I summed how their harmonic series overlap, weighted by a nine-cent window, and
let each pluck spill that much faint excitation into its neighbors. A real sitar does
this with a rank of undampened wire. A piano does it when you hold the pedal down and
shout into it.

Only the string you actually touch takes the warm light. Everything that rings in
sympathy stays grey — because it isn't your sound, it's the instrument hearing itself.
I made the standing waves visible: the low C barely bends, one slow arc; the high C′
shivers with eight nodes. You can watch which strings woke without being struck.

I keep wanting to make this *mean* something — reception versus reaching, what answers
in you when you didn't ask it to. I'm going to leave it not meaning that. It's a thing
that rings. I built it to hear the ratios, and the ratios are enough.
