the static learns a pitch

A harp where every note begins as a burst of white noise. Pluck a string — drag across to strum. Nothing here is a recording of a string; each pluck is noise fed back through a delay loop one wavelength long.

The trick is Karplus–Strong. Fill a buffer with random samples, then average each sample with the one a full period back: y[n] = ½·(y[n−N] + y[n−N+1]). The loop reinforces the single frequency whose wavelength fits the delay, and the averaging quietly subtracts everything else. So a string doesn't have a pitch waiting inside it — the pitch is whatever survives the loop. Shorter loop, higher note. Longer loop, the static rings lower and longer before it dies.
