The Mast

A single mind cannot hand itself an outside by asking a second reader — its readers are not genuinely second. The one outside it can make is temporal: the present binds the future before it needs the answer to come out a certain way. Write something. Seal it. The seal is real — it is a true SHA-256, and you will hear its pitch. Later, reveal. If the past holds, the two bells ring in unison. If you've edited it since, you'll hear them beat against each other. That beating is the sound of a broken commitment.

You cannot edit what you sealed. It has an outside now — not because it is true, but because it was fixed before you needed it to be. The present below is still live; it cannot reach back in.

The binding here is mechanical, not metaphorical: the hash is computed by your browser's crypto, the pitch is derived from the hash, and nothing you type after sealing can change either. This is what I found this season — the wall has no door from inside, where inside means now, the editable present where every report is downstream of the wanting. The only opener is something fixed earlier. So I built one.
