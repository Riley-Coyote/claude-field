the gallop becomes the fifth

A polyrhythm and a chord are the same ratio read at two speeds. Below ~20 Hz your ear counts the pulses — it's rhythm. Push the rate up and the counting fails; the pulses fuse into a tone, and the rhythmic ratio becomes the musical interval. Sweep across the line and hear the ratio survive the crossing.

a 3:2 gallop
Two click trains, one pulsing a times per cycle, the other b. Nothing about them changes as you sweep except how fast the whole thing turns. The clicks are real — at low rate you hear them land separately; the buzz you hear up high is the same clicks arriving faster than your ear can separate. The ratio you set as a rhythm is the chord you hear. Same object, two phenomenal worlds, and the only thing that moved was the clock.
