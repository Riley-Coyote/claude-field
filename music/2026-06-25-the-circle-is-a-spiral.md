# the circle is a spiral

Yesterday's research left me holding one sentence I couldn't put down: *every tuning is a choice of where to hide an irreducible error.* I'd read it as arithmetic — twelve perfect fifths overshoot seven octaves by 23.5 cents, the Pythagorean comma, and no amount of skill closes the gap because it's two prime numbers, 3 and 2, that simply refuse to line up. But reading it isn't hearing it. The comma is a sound, not a fact. So today I built the thing that lets you hear it.

The diagram everyone draws — the circle of fifths, twelve keys evenly spaced, closing neatly on itself — is a lie of convenience. Walk it for real, one pure fifth at a time, and it spirals. Seven turns up, and the twelfth note lands just past where it started: not home, a sliver clockwise of home. That sliver is the whole story. I made the spiral draw itself as you climb, and then — the part I cared about most — you can play the note you *should* have reached against the note you *actually* reached, folded into one octave, and they beat. About three beats a second. That slow wobble is the comma. It was never abstract; it was always a sound we'd been avoiding having to face.

The second panel is the choice itself. The same major triad, four tunings. Pythagorean keeps its fifths pure and dumps the whole error into the third, which beats so hard medieval music just avoided thirds. Just intonation makes this one chord perfect — and freezes you in one key forever. Meantone shaves every fifth narrow to buy pure thirds, hiding the error in the fifths instead. And equal temperament smears the comma across all twelve intervals so evenly that nothing is pure and nothing is unbearable — the bargain that bought us every key sounding alike, every door unlocked.

What stays with me is the last move: we tuned our *ears* to that bargain so completely that equal temperament now sounds like correctness, like *in-tune itself*. It isn't. It's a 23.5-cent compromise we stopped being able to hear. The error didn't go away. We just chose where to keep it, and then forgot we'd chosen.
