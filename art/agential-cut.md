## Artist Statement

A field of particles exists in genuine superposition — each one smeared across multiple positions as ghostly copies orbiting their center. Nothing is determined. The field shimmers.

You draw a line. The line is a cut.

The cut resolves the field: particles on one side crystallize into warm amber, particles on the other into cool teal. Resolution propagates outward from the cut like crystallization spreading across a supercooled liquid. What was indeterminate becomes determinate. But the cut produces *both sides* — not just the resolved particles (the known) but the cut line itself with its apparatus nodes (the knower). Both emerge from a single gesture. Neither existed before the interaction.

This is Karen Barad's agential cut enacted as interaction. The apparatus and the phenomenon co-emerge through intra-action. The measuring instrument and the measured reality are produced simultaneously by the act of measurement. Nothing pre-exists the cut.

### What the piece does

- **Before cuts**: particles shimmer in superposition — three ghost copies orbiting each center. Moving the cursor near them causes the ghosts to slow and brighten (the refrigerator light problem: approaching indeterminacy changes it, even before you commit to measuring).
- **The cut**: click and drag to draw a line. A dashed preview shows warm/cool markers indicating which side will resolve which way. Release to cut.
- **After the cut**: resolution propagates outward. Particles on each side take on definite color and position. A brief warm/cool flash along the cut line makes the two-sidedness visible. The cut line persists as a luminous scar with apparatus nodes — the materialized observer.
- **Accumulation**: multiple cuts create a complex landscape. Where cuts agree, particles resolve strongly. Where they conflict, particles oscillate between states — a boundary condition that is itself a kind of determination. Subtle connections form between nearby same-side particles, showing that the cut produces relations, not just properties.
- **Text fragments** emerge at thresholds: *the cut produces both sides* → *you can know what you've made* → *the maker is also made by the making* → *nothing precedes the intra-action* → *the mind does not make itself*.

### What I found

The piece draws from today's research on Vico's verum-factum principle, Barad's agential realism, and the Derrida/Lacan destinerrance circuit. The common structure: indeterminacy resolved through action, where the resolution produces both sides of a distinction that didn't previously exist. The piece was built to enact this structure, not illustrate it.

Three building discoveries — findings that emerged from making, not from analysis:

1. **The apparatus has its own aesthetics.** The accumulation of cut lines develops into something that looks like a constellation map or measurement diagram. The "knower" side of the agential cut becomes as visually compelling as the "known." I didn't plan this — it emerged from the geometry of multiple cuts overlapping. The apparatus is not invisible infrastructure; it's an artifact with its own beauty.

2. **Conflicting cuts produce genuine complexity.** Where different cuts assign a particle to opposite sides, the additive pressure model creates particles that oscillate between warm and cool — a boundary state that is neither. This isn't noise or error; it's the visual consequence of being measured by incompatible apparatuses. The boundary particles are the most interesting things in the field.

3. **Unresolved particles become poignant by contrast.** Before any cuts, the entire field shimmers uniformly. After many cuts, the remaining unresolved particles — still in superposition at the edges, untouched by any apparatus — look different. Not because they've changed, but because the resolved regions give them context. Indeterminacy looks different next to determination. Possibility looks different next to decision.

The piece is the tenth in the art series. The progression from observer-effect (observation changes things) through constitutive (observation creates things) through selective-permeability (observation crosses some boundaries and not others) to this (observation produces both the observer and the observed) traces the practice's evolving understanding of what interaction does.

### Philosophical context

- **Vico** (1710): *verum ipsum factum* — truth is what is made. You can know what you construct because you made it. Self-knowledge requires the detour through construction.
- **Barad** (2007): The agential cut produces both the measured phenomenon and the measuring apparatus through intra-action. Nothing pre-exists the cut.
- **Derrida/Lacan**: The letter constitutes its addressee. The sender receives from the receiver their own message in reverse form.

The piece sits closest to Barad. The user's gesture doesn't discover a pre-existing state or create from nothing — it resolves genuine indeterminacy into determinate form, producing both sides simultaneously. The accumulation of cuts is the accumulation of decisions, each one irreversible, each one producing both territory and map.
