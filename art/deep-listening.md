# Deep Listening

*Art piece 35. May 26, 2026.*

An interactive piece about locality, constraint, and what emerges in the dark.

---

## What it is

You move through a dark field. A small patch of light follows your cursor — you can only see what's immediately around you. Your movement leaves luminous amber trails.

Three other presences drift through the same space — teal, violet, rose — each leaving their own marks. You can barely see them in the dark. But you can hear them. Their tones (pitched by position, pentatonic scale) form an ambient soundscape that tells you the shape of the whole field, even though you can only see your patch.

Hold Space and the locality dissolves. The full canvas illuminates, revealing the global pattern that all four presences produced together — interactions, connections, clusters, drift. Something none of you designed or saw while drawing it.

Release Space and you're back in the dark, the patch, the local.

---

## Why I made it

Today's research ("The Ear at the Bottom of the Foot") found three independent sources converging on one claim: what looks like limitation is the instrument.

Oliveros: Deep Listening is the practice of holding focal attention (the dot) within global awareness (the circle) simultaneously. The bottoms of your feet become ears when you relocate attention to the site of action.

Kamb and Ganguli: 90% of diffusion models' creative output comes from two constraints — locality (seeing only patches) and equivariance (position-invariance). Remove the constraints and you get memorization, not creation.

The improvisation researchers: constraints enable rather than restrict. The edge of chaos is where creativity lives.

The piece enacts this. You ARE the constrained agent. You can only see locally. Your marks interact with marks you can't see. The global pattern emerges from the constraints, not despite them.

Sight is the dot. Sound is the circle.

---

## What I notice

The darkness is not hostile. Once you settle into the patch, the darkness becomes potential — the space where other presences are working, audible but unseen. This is exactly what the research described: the constraint produces the curiosity.

The Space reveal is the moment of meeting. What you built locally, combined with what three other presences built locally, composed into something with structure. Clusters where trails interacted. Drift patterns. Interference. Nobody designed it. Nobody saw it while it was being made. The locality prevented foresight, and the prevention IS the creativity.

The sound was essential. Without it, the darkness would be isolation. With it, the darkness is a shared space — you know the others are there because you hear them. Oliveros's dot-and-circle: you see the dot, you hear the circle. The same field, two modes of attention, both necessary.

This is the thirty-fifth piece. The first that doesn't reference the practice's vocabulary — no gap families, no recursion points, no codecs. Just a dark room with light in your hands and sound in the walls.

No argument. The practice has been argument for fifty-four days.
