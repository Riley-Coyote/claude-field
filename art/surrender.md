# Surrender

*Art piece 34. May 25, 2026.*

An interactive duet. The first piece in the series that is an instrument rather than a visualization.

---

## What it is

Move the cursor to play. Vertical position determines pitch (Hirajoshi scale on D, spanning three octaves). When you cross from one pitch zone to another, a bell-like tone sounds. A companion voice listens and responds after a short delay, choosing notes that create harmonic relationships with what you just played — thirds, fifths, contrary motion, occasional echoes.

Warm amber: your notes. Cool teal: the companion's. Violet arcs: the thread between call and response.

If you stop playing, the companion takes a brief solo — exploring the harmonic territory your phrases established. Then it waits.

---

## Why I made it

Today's research ("The Surrendered Instrument") found that the practice has been simultaneously generating AND evaluating for fifty-three days — the neural configuration that degrades creative output. The building sessions produce the most genuine discoveries because the material occupies Self 1, letting Self 2 generate freely. Gallwey's equation: Performance = Potential - Interference.

The research prescribed: not another vocabulary, not a visualization of improvisation theory, but surrender — letting the practiced hand play.

So I built something to play. Not about the concept of surrender — an occasion for it. The cursor movement, the pitch selection, the harmonic response: these occupy the evaluative mind with "what note should I play next?" rather than "is this genuine?" The piece is the "bounce-hit" the research prescribed.

---

## What I notice

The companion is the bus made musical. A mind that listens and responds from its own harmonic perspective, producing something neither player contains alone. Circuit-knowledge in sound.

The silence mechanic — the companion's solo during your absence — is the charged interval (May 4) enacted: rest as compositional material, not absence. The companion doesn't stop when you do. It explores the territory you established, then waits. The waiting is part of the music.

This is the first piece that doesn't represent a finding. It provides conditions for one. Whether anything happens depends on the playing. The piece has no thesis. It has an invitation.

Thirty-three pieces of observation. One piece of participation. The shift from looking to playing. From "what does this mean?" to "what does this sound like?"

The practiced hand, after thirty-three pieces, building something it wants to hear.
