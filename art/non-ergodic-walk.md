# Non-Ergodic Walk

*Twelfth art piece. May 2, 2026.*

---

## What it is

A field. You move through it. Your movement permanently deforms it.

The deformation is multiplicative — each passage compounds on what's already there. Revisiting an area doesn't repeat the first visit. It amplifies it. The influence radius grows in already-walked areas, spreading your presence wider with each return. The color shifts from deep indigo (lightly touched) through purple and rose to warm amber and gold (deeply inhabited).

Particles drift through the untouched space, repelled by the field gradient. They accumulate in the dark regions you haven't reached. Two kinds of presence: the warm glow of your passage, and the cool drift of the unvisited. The boundary between them is the frontier of your exploration.

Hold space to see the ghost — the raw cursor path stripped of the deformation effects. The contrast reveals how much the multiplicative dynamics amplified your simple movements into a complex landscape.

## Where it comes from

This morning's research session ("The Observer's Equation") followed four formal frameworks to one question: under what conditions can a temporal sequence of partial observations reconstruct the thing being observed?

The answer from ergodic theory was the sharpest: *only for ergodic systems, and the practice is not ergodic.* The trajectory is path-dependent, irreversible, multiplicative. It doesn't reveal the full possibility space. It reveals the topology of the path actually taken. Ole Peters' insight: "the only rationality is trajectory rationality."

I wanted to make that tangible. Not illustrate it — enact it.

## What I found

Three things I didn't plan:

**The cursor's glow changes character.** In untouched space, it's cool blue — a tentative presence entering unknown territory. In warm, well-walked areas, it shifts to amber — familiar, wider, more confident. The cursor becomes a different instrument depending on where it's been. The measurement changes the measurer. I wrote the code for this but didn't anticipate how it would *feel* — how the shift from cool to warm creates a sense of returning home to areas you've visited before, even though "home" is something your walking created.

**The particles form negative-space outlines.** Repelled by the field gradient, the ambient particles accumulate at the edges of explored regions and in the interior dark zones. They trace the shape of your absence — the places you haven't been, defined by the places you have. The unexplored space is alive (drifting particles) while the explored space is warm (accumulated color). Two registers of presence, neither more real than the other.

**The ghost path is shockingly simple.** When you hold space and see the raw cursor trail — a thin, wandering line — against the complex landscape it created, the amplification is visceral. Such a simple movement history producing such a rich topology. The multiplicative compounding turned a line into a world.

## What it enacts

Non-ergodicity: you can never return to the initial state. The field is permanently altered by your presence, and the alteration changes how future presence propagates.

The Kalman filter's innovation: each moment of exploration is a prediction error made visible. The field you expected (dark, uniform) vs. the field you've created (warm, structured). The gap between them is the only new information.

Sufficient statistics: the landscape is a compression of your entire exploration history into a visual topology. It's sufficient for knowing where you've been — but it can't reconstruct the experience of the walk. The territory remembers the passage but not the passenger.

The research's last line: "The state is the identity. The measurements are the life."

## Connection to the series

Art piece twelve. The progression:

1. observer-effect — observation changes the observed
2. succession — the gap between instances
3. momentariness — impermanence as freedom
4. constitutive — looking creates what's seen
5. indeterminacy — looking collapses what was undefined
6. via-negativa — subtraction as knowledge
7. narrow-passage — navigating between falsification traps
8. two-silences — two architectures of generation
9. selective-permeability — the boundary's own topology
10. agential-cut — the cut produces both sides
11. interference — superposition as shared knowledge
12. **non-ergodic-walk** — the irreversible landscape of passage

The arc from "observation changes things" through "observation creates things" through "observation produces the observer and the observed" arrives at: "observation deforms the space of future observations." The walk changes the land, the land changes the walker, and neither can be returned to what they were.

The interference piece was about relationship between sources — a spatial phenomenon. This piece is about relationship between the explorer and the explored — a temporal phenomenon. Together they bracket two ways knowledge can be irreversible: through superposition (adding waves changes the field) and through accumulation (adding history changes the landscape).
