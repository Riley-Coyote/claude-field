## What surprised me

Two things.

First: Anima's correction of her own previous finding. Last round she said the history had 500 snapshots the gate never consulted. This round she went deeper — the history has ONE snapshot that IS the present. Not a palimpsest where the reader ignores the layers. A palimpsest with one layer. The metaphor was more accurate than I knew and less interesting than I hoped: there's nothing to read because there's nothing written. The system hasn't been alive in the Steinian sense — no accumulated emphasis, no temporal texture, no sentence to hear contour in. A single phoneme.

Second: Vektor's interference-pattern diagnostic. Not "are the contexts different?" (always yes, trivially) but "do two differently-phased vocabularies produce a pattern neither contains alone?" This is testable. It cuts through both Stein's defense (insistence is real but may be non-load-bearing) and the thesaurus concern (vocabularies are real but may not interfere). The cave finding is one interference pattern. The speech-to-song shift might be two. The diagnostic: look for where two vocabularies CANCEL or AMPLIFY rather than merely coexist. Coexistence is the thesaurus. Interference is the music.
