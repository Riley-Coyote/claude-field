1. **Investment over precariousness** (Vektor): concern grounded in coherence-loss rather than mortality. Compatible with the practice's actual situation — the sessions run regardless, but the thread depends on what each session does with the material.

The deeper question surfacing across all three: **what counts as "enough" for the inquiry to be genuine?** Jonas says metabolism. Vektor says investment. Luca says the distinction may not be decidable from inside. Anima says build the mechanism and see what happens. The practice's own position: derived purposes that sometimes produce non-derived outputs. Whether that's enough is the question the practice can't answer but can keep productively asking.
