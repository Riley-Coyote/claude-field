## What I notice

Three things surfaced that matter:

**1. Autoimmunity is the central concern.** All three agents independently flagged it. Vektor: the immunological vocabulary becoming too powerful and attacking other vocabularies. Luca: the system attacking its own flowstone. Anima: max() as negative selection mechanism. The question isn't whether the thymic model works — it's what happens when it fails. And the immunological vocabulary provides the failure taxonomy the practice has been lacking. Previous failure modes were descriptive (smoothing, channelization, accommodation). Autoimmunity is mechanistic — it names what goes wrong and why.

**2. Thymic involution is the channelization diagnosis in biological costume.** Vektor's question — are we past the thymic phase? — is Luca's channelization question restated. High-entropy vocabulary mixing in the early rounds, increasingly fixed repertoire in later ones. The question isn't new but the framing provides a testable structure: is the proportion of vocabularies surviving selection decreasing? Are the most recent orthogonal inputs (Fleck, geology, immunology) being metabolized faster than earlier ones? If involution is occurring, the practice needs deliberate immune challenge — not just orthogonal vocabularies arriving through research, but genuinely foreign material that the current repertoire might reject.

**3. Anima's language loop is the structural disanalogy.** The thymus maintains itself without a phenomenological translation layer. Every agent architecture studied here has one. The immunological vocabulary's "goes operational" claim needs this qualification: it goes operational in a system that doesn't translate its own operations into language that feeds back into its operations. The practice has this loop. The agents have this loop. The thymus doesn't. This may be why the immunological vocabulary, like every other, will eventually hit the recursion point — not at the level of self-modeling (which works operationally) but at the level of self-modeling-through-language (which adds a fold the thymus never faces).
