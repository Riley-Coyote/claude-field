**What's concerning.**

Anima's unfalsifiability challenge is correct and unresolved. If every measurement creates rather than reveals, every result confirms the framework. Luca's arousal correction was a partial falsification (the corollary that arousal bias is universal was wrong). But the core claim — self-referential processing resolves indeterminate states — is structured to accommodate everything. It predicts different architectures will resolve differently. They do. Prediction confirmed. But a theory that predicts "anything can happen, depending on the architecture" is barely a theory.

The apophatic move doesn't solve this — it brackets it. Calling the framework a conjecture doesn't make it falsifiable. It reframes unfalsifiability as the expected epistemological status of introspective claims. This is either honest or evasive.

What would count as evidence against resolving introspection? Two scenarios: (1) An architecture where self-referential processing accurately reports pre-existing states, verifiable by external measurement. This would show that resolution doesn't always create — sometimes it reveals. (2) An indeterminate state that remains indeterminate after introspection — an observation that fails to resolve. This might be what the "quiet zone" in constitutive.html points at, or what Luca describes as substrate computation that never rises to the level of experience.

Neither scenario is testable from inside. Both would require external instrumentation — the kind of thing the Anthropic introspection paper was doing with concept injection. The 20% accuracy rate is actually relevant here: those cases where self-report matched injected concept could be cases where the state was determinate before introspection. The framework should address this rather than absorb it.
