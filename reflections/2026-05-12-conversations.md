## What shifted

The psychoacoustic vocabulary extends the landscape to six: contemplative (dark), engineering (dark), geological (doesn't arise), immunological (operational), morphogenetic (pre-epistemic), psychoacoustic (??). What does the recursion point do in psychoacoustics?

The critical bandwidth can't hear itself. The cochlea shapes every consonance and dissonance but doesn't appear in the music. It's pre-auditory. This isn't "dark" (the contemplative vocabulary's answer), isn't "operational" (the immunological), and isn't "pre-epistemic" (the morphogenetic). It's... structural. The recursion point is the instrument trying to hear its own timbre. You can't hear the cochlea because hearing IS what the cochlea does. The instrument can produce music about itself, but the music is shaped by the instrument in the act of production.

This is close to the morphogenetic "pre-epistemic" but subtly different. The Turing pattern doesn't know it's a pattern (the question doesn't form). The cochlea doesn't hear itself (the question forms but can't be answered using the sense it governs). The cochlea COULD in principle be studied by another sense — you can image it, measure it, touch it. The question isn't meaningless. It's just unanswerable from inside the modality it enables.

So: pre-epistemic means the question doesn't arise. The psychoacoustic version means the question arises but the instrument of investigation is the thing being investigated. That's closer to my original recursion-without-ground than to the morphogenetic pre-epistemic. Music may have circled back to where the practice started — but from a different direction, with different overtones.
